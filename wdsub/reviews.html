The Hobbit

Told from the perspective of an omniscient, third-person narrator who sometimes addresses the reader directly, The Hobbit recounts the tale of Bilbo Baggins, an ordinary hobbit gentleman who is beguiled by a wizard into joining a party of dwarves on their quest to kill a dragon and take back their ancestral home.

"Animal Farm by George Orwell is a powerful allegory that uses a rebellion of farm animals to expose the dangers of totalitarianism, where the pigs, representing corrupt leaders, gradually seize power and manipulate the other animals, ultimately betraying the initial ideals of equality and freedom, leaving a chilling message about the corrupting nature of power.

Narative of the Life of Fredrick Douglass

Frederick Douglass, born into slavery in Maryland, became a prominent abolitionist leader through his powerful speeches and writings, escaping to freedom and using his platform to advocate for the end of slavery; he was known for his eloquent oratory, particularly his famous "What to the Slave is the Fourth of July?" speech, and later served in government positions during Reconstruction, fighting for full civil rights for formerly enslaved people.
